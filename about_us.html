About College

SKU College of ARTS

Sri Krishnadevaraya University, Ananthapuramu, had its beginning as a Postgraduate Centre of Sri Venkateswara University in the year 1967-68. Fulfilling the desires of the people of the region, the Autonomous Postgraduate Centre was accorded the status of a University in the year 1981. Evolved over the decades and in 2015 SKU College has divided into two colleges of which SKU College of Arts is one. SKU College of Arts has 13 Departments offering 18 PG courses besides one UG course.



SKU College of Science

Fulfilling the desires of the people of the region, the Autonomous Postgraduate Centre was accorded the status of a University in the year 1981. Evolved over the decades and in 2015 SKU College has divided into two colleges of which SKU College of Sciences is one. SKU College of Sciences has 16 Departments offering 19 PG courses.



College of Engineering and Technology

Sri Krishnadevaraya University College of Engineering & Technology came into existence in the year 2006 to mark the silver jubilee celebrations of the university. It was established to carter to the needs of the technical education of the young generation of the region. It offers Bachelor of Technology in 5 engineering branches Biotechnology, Civil Engineering, Computer Science and Engineering, Electronics and Communication Engineering and Electrical and Electronics Enginering. And Master of Technology in 3 courses Computer Science and Engineering, Electrical and Power Systems and Embedded Systems and VLSI Design from the academic year 2013-2014.

Brief History of the Department :



The Computer Science Programme namely Post Graduate Diploma in Computer Science and Applications sanctioned by UGC ,was instituted in the year 1989 under the Co-ordinatorship of Prof K. N. Venkatasivamurthy, Dept. of Mathematics. Later in the year 1991, the Course was run in collaboration with the Computer centre, Head of Computer centre, Mr C Lokanatha Reddy was In- Charge of the Programme. Subsequently in the year 1994 the Post Graduate Courses like M.C.A and M.Sc(Computer Science) were instituted as Self-funding Courses. As per the directions of University Grants Commission the PGDCSA course was converted into M.C.A Course, with the Equipment and staff appointed for PGDCSA Course were automatically placed under the M.C.A course. June 2001 the Department of Computer Science and Applications has been separated from University Computer centre as per University Grants Commission Guidelines. The department has its own facilities namely staff, equipment, buildings etc. April 2004 the Department of Computer Science and Applications and University Computer centre were merged and named as Computer Science & Technology.
